Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before getting started. Can't find your answer? Give us a call.

How long does a tarmac driveway last?

A properly laid tarmac driveway can last 15–25 years with minimal maintenance. The key is proper sub-base preparation and quality materials — both of which we take very seriously on every job. Regular sealing every 5–7 years will extend the life even further.

Do you offer free quotes?

Yes, absolutely. All quotes from Munster Pave & Drive are completely free, detailed and come with no obligation whatsoever. We'll visit your property, assess the job properly and provide a transparent, itemised quote so you know exactly what you're getting.

How long does it take to lay a driveway?

Most residential tarmac driveways are completed in 1–2 days depending on size and complexity. Block paving and resin driveways may take slightly longer due to the nature of the materials. We'll give you a clear timeline before we start and stick to it.

Do I need planning permission for a new driveway?

In most cases, no planning permission is required for a standard residential driveway in Ireland. However, if your property is in a protected structure area or if you're creating a new vehicular entrance from a public road, permission may be needed. Resin and permeable surfaces are generally the safest choice from a planning perspective. We can advise on this during your free quote visit.

We cover all of Limerick, Clare, Kerry, Cork and Tipperary, plus surrounding areas. We regularly travel throughout Munster and have completed jobs as far as Waterford and Galway. If you're not sure whether we cover your area, just give us a call or send us a message — we're happy to travel for the right job.

How soon can you start?

We typically have availability within 2–4 weeks of a confirmed booking. During busier periods (spring and summer) lead times can be slightly longer. We always aim to be as flexible as possible and will work around your schedule where we can. Contact us today to check current availability.

Is tarmac or resin better for my driveway?

Both are excellent options and the right choice depends on your budget, aesthetic preference and any planning requirements. Tarmac is more cost-effective, quick to install and very durable — perfect for most homes. Resin offers more decorative options, is fully permeable and looks premium — ideal for those who want a standout finish. We'll help you choose the best option for your specific situation during your free quote.

Are you fully insured?

Yes, we carry full public liability insurance on every job we undertake. We're happy to provide proof of insurance on request. Our insurance covers all works carried out by our teams, giving you complete peace of mind throughout the entire project.
